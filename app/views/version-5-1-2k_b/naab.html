Terms of use

This service can only be used to view and sign a deed, using an electronic signature supplied by the Chief Land Registrar (‘Land Registry’).

To use this service, you must:

  • be at least 18 years old
  • agree to keep your password and other security details safe and not share them with anyone else
  • have effective security and virus protection on the computer or device you’re using to access the service

Tell Land Registry (by calling 0300 006 0411) if you think someone else has your security details.

This agreement only exists between you and Land Registry while you use this service to view and sign a deed. It stops automatically if you die or become bankrupt during this time.

Land Registry can stop providing this service at any time if it believes this action is necessary.

Land Registry may change the terms of this agreement but they will not change whilst you are viewing or signing your deed.

Your liability

You will have to compensate Land Registry for any loss and damage it suffers as a direct result of you:

  • misusing this service, either intentionally, recklessly or through negligence
  • otherwise acting negligently
  • using the service without meeting the conditions in this agreement
  • misrepresenting your identity

You will also have to compensate Land Registry if someone else makes a claim against it for loss or damage they suffer as a direct result of these actions, but that compensation will be reduced if Land Registry has caused any of the loss.

Land Registry’s liability

When you use this service, this agreement does not give Land Registry a duty of care or contractual obligation to anyone else.

Where it is at fault, Land Registry’s standard legal requirement to pay compensation for losses or damages doesn’t change.

There is no limit on compensation for:

  • death or personal injury resulting from Land Registry’s negligence, or
  • acts or omissions it makes in bad faith

Additionally, Land Registry will compensate you for costs and expenses you incur as a direct result if it acts wrongly under the law, provided these total more than £120 but less than £250.

It doesn’t have to pay compensation for losses or damages caused by:

  • any action it takes as the registrar, by law or which it is entitled to make according to this agreement
  • you breaking the terms of this agreement or the law, unless that happened because Land Registry broke this agreement or failed in its duties
  • loss or corruption of information on your computer or device
  • any damage caused to your computer or device as a result of you failing to provide up-to-date anti-virus measures software
  • any damage caused by you or anyone else deliberately altering the service or introducing a virus, worm or other malignant software

Land Registry’s employees and agents are not personally liable for any loss or damage you or anyone else suffers, unless they have acted in bad faith.

Land Registry is not liable for inaccurate information supplied by the service except where compensation is required by law.
